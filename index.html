French wines

France can produce a large quantity of wines, all as different as each other. This diversity of wines, it owes to the multiple terroirs, diverse and varied, making up its ten wine-growing areas. But it also owes it to the permanent and constant work of men and women who support and protect the entire wine heritage formed by the many appellations and names (AOC-AOP and IGP). They can be present in red wines, in white wines, in rosé wines or in other colors, amber, tuilée, rancio, yellow ... In order to organize this multitude of wines, the national institute of origin and quality, under the supervision of the Ministry of Agriculture of the French Republic, has classified the wines by appellations and denominations, by color and by category, for example: Champagne grand cru and Champagne premier cru are names of the Champagne appellation.
